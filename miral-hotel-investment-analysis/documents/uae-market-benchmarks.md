## 5. Chain-Scale Benchmarks — UAE (2024 Reference)

| Chain Scale | Typical Occupancy | Typical ADR (AED) | GOP Margin | Labour % Revenue |
|-------------|------------------|--------------------|------------|-----------------|
| Luxury / Upper Upscale (5★) | 70–80% | AED 800–1,500+ | 35–45% | 28–33% |
| Upscale (4★ full-service) | 72–82% | AED 450–800 | 30–38% | 30–35% |
| Upper Midscale (4★ select) | 75–85% | AED 300–500 | 32–40% | 25–30% |
| Midscale / Economy (3★) | 75–85% | AED 200–350 | 28–35% | 22–28% |
| Serviced Apartments | 78–88% | AED 250–600 | 30–40% | 18–24% |

**Departmental margin benchmarks (UAE full-service):**
- Rooms department: 72–80% (target)
- F&B: 22–32% (lower than global avg due to UAE alcohol licensing costs, Ramadan impact)
- Spa: 30–42%
- FF&E reserve: 4% of gross revenue (industry standard); below 3% = flag

**OTA mix benchmarks:**
- Healthy: 15–22% of transient revenue
- Caution: 25–30% — rate integrity risk
- Flag: >30% — commission drag, rate parity issues

## 6. Seasonality Patterns — Abu Dhabi / Yas Island

| Month | Season | Typical Occ | Driver | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------------|--------|-------|
| Jan | High | 80–90% | Post-holiday leisure, events | New Year momentum |
| Feb | High | 82–92% | IDEX (biennial), business travel | IDEX years spike ADR dramatically |
| Mar | High | 78–88% | Spring break, GCC families | |
| Apr | High/Shoulder | 72–82% | Eid al-Fitr (varies) | Ramadan affects F&B revenue |
| May | Shoulder | 65–75% | Ramadan, shoulder | Quietest shoulder month |
| Jun | Low/Shoulder | 58–68% | Start of summer; Eid al-Adha (varies) | Eid spike possible |
| Jul | Low | 55–68% | Summer heat; school holidays | Miral "Kids Go Free" campaign |
| Aug | Low/Peak for Yas | 70–90%* | School holidays; Miral peak | *Yas Island bucks trend — WB Hotel 92% Aug 2024 |
| Sep | Shoulder | 62–72% | Back-to-school; quiet | |
| Oct | High | 78–88% | Season reopens; F1 build-up | |
| Nov | Peak | 88–98% | F1 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix | Highest ADR month of year |
| Dec | High | 82–90% | Christmas, New Year build | Long-stay GCC + international |

**F&B seasonality note**: Ramadan (dates vary annually) suppresses F&B revenue by 30–50% in hotels
with liquor licences. Non-alcohol F&B typically increases. Model separately.

**Yas Island summer anomaly**: Unlike most UAE hotels, Yas Island maintains strong summer
occupancy due to school holiday theme park demand, GCC domestic families, and Miral's
summer activation campaigns. This is a structural competitive advantage vs. downtown hotels.
